What is a network ?

A network is a bunch of things that are exchanging other things. Yeah, this definition seems useless, but it's actually the most precise. Every time there is an exchange there is a network. For example we could consider the network of cities in Germany as people and merchandises travel from one to the other. But you are futur computer scientist so let's have a look at information networks (where the thing exchanged is information).

One of the oldest information network is the network of alert fires build by the romans (and the chineses yes...). It was a really simple network with only 1 bit of information : fire means attack, no fire means no attack.

There are two possible actions with the elements of this network: light a fire or not. The convention that links the action to a meaning (attack or not) as well as the procedure to observe to use the network (when I see a fire or an attack, I light my fire) is called a protocol.

A protocol is just a convention to know what to do and understand what we see.
